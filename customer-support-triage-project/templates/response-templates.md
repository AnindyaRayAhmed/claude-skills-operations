# Draft Response Templates

Seven ready-to-use templates covering the most common support scenarios.
Customize bracketed fields before sending. Never send placeholder text as-is.

---

## 1. Empathy-First Opening
**Use for:** ESCALATED emotional state, SENTIMENT SCORE 1–2

```
I'm really sorry to hear about your experience with [issue]. That's not the standard
we hold ourselves to, and I want to make sure this gets the attention it deserves.
```

**Note:** Always follow with a concrete next step. Do not use this opener in isolation —
pair it immediately with action or it reads as hollow.

---

## 2. Acknowledgement + Action (Standard)
**Use for:** MEDIUM urgency, NEUTRAL or FRUSTRATED state, first contact

```
Thank you for getting in touch. I can see you're experiencing [issue summary] and I
want to help resolve this as quickly as possible. I've [action taken / escalated to
relevant team] and will [next step — be specific].
```

---

## 3. Information-Gathering Request
**Use for:** CONFIDENCE = LOW or INSUFFICIENT, missing key details

```
Thank you for reaching out. To make sure I can look into this properly, could you
share [specific missing information — e.g., your order number / account email / a
screenshot of the error]? Once I have that, I'll [what happens next — be concrete].
```

---

## 4. Escalation Notice (to Customer)
**Use for:** Cases routed to specialist teams; HIGH or CRITICAL urgency

```
I've flagged your case to our [specialist team / senior team] who handles [issue type].
They'll be reviewing it as a priority. In the meantime, your reference number is [REF]
— please quote this in any future contact.

[Only add timeframe if you can confirm it: "You can expect to hear back within [X]."]
```

---

## 5. Billing / Charge Review (Non-Committal)
**Use for:** Refund requests, disputed charges — before investigation is complete

```
Thank you for flagging this. I've raised a review of your account for this charge with
our billing team. They'll assess and you'll hear back [within our standard timeframe /
by [date] — only include if confirmed]. We appreciate your patience while we look into this.
```

**Do not use:** "You'll receive a refund" or "We'll reverse the charge" — until confirmed.

---

## 6. Social Media (Public — Move to Private)
**Use for:** Any public social post requiring support action

```
Hi [name / @handle], we're really sorry to hear this. We'd love to sort it out for
you — please send us a DM with your order or account details and we'll get on it
straight away.
```

**Keep public replies under 3 sentences. Resolve in private.**

---

## 7. Closing (Email Sign-Off)
**Use for:** End of email responses

```
Please don't hesitate to reach back out if there's anything else I can help with.
We value your relationship with us and want to make this right.

Warm regards,
[Agent Name]
[Team Name / Company]
[Contact details if applicable]
```

---

## Usage Notes

- **Never hardcode** refund amounts, fix timelines, or policy commitments unless 100% confirmed with internal teams.
- **Channel tone**: Email = formal + full sign-off. WhatsApp/Chat = conversational, no formal sign-off needed. Social = brief + move to DM.
- **ESCALATED customers**: Always lead with Template 1 before any other template.
- **GDPR / Legal**: Use Template 4 (Escalation Notice) only — do not engage on substance. Route to DPO/legal immediately.
