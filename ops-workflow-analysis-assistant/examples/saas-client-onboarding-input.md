# SaaS Client Onboarding — Input

**Context provided to Claude:**

---

Our client onboarding is honestly kind of a disaster. Here's roughly how it works:

When sales closes a deal, they send an email to the ops team letting them know. The email usually has the client name, the plan they signed up for, and sometimes a note about special requirements — but not always. Ops then has to go into Salesforce to find the rest of the details, create the account in our platform manually, and send a welcome email to the client.

After that, ops tries to schedule the kickoff call by emailing the client and going back and forth until they find a time that works. This usually takes a few days. Once the kickoff is scheduled, they assign a Customer Success Manager — I'm not totally sure how they decide who gets assigned, I think it's just whoever has capacity at the time.

The CSM then reviews the Salesforce record before the kickoff and usually has to ask ops a few clarifying questions because the notes are incomplete.

We're doing maybe 15–20 new clients a month right now and it's manageable but we're planning to scale and I can tell this will break. The main complaints I hear are that clients sometimes wait 2–3 days before hearing from us after signing, and our CSMs feel like they're going into kickoffs underprepared.

What's broken and where should we start?
